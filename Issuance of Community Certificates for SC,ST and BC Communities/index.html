ISSUANCE OF SCHEDULED CASTES, SCHEDULED TRIBES
& BACKWARD CLASSES CERTIFICATES

INTRODUCTION:

Community Certificates are issued to the Candidates who are seeking employment in Govt. Dept./PSUs and
Candidates seeking admission into educational institutions against the vacancies/seats reserved for SC, ST, BC.
Issuance of false Community Certificates deprives the reservation benefits of above classes and care must
be taken to curb such practices and it is dire need to study the guidelines/provisions before issuing Caste Certificates

LEGAL FRAME WORK:

The issuance of the caste certificates to the SC, ST & BC communities are Governed by
the following guidelines issued by central and state Governments.

ANDHRA PRADESH STATE ACT & RULES:

The Govt. of Andhra Pradesh enacted the following Act, and framed Rules by introducing the system of issuance of
permanent and integrated community certificate to the SC, ST & BC Communities.

  1. The Andhra Pradesh (Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Backward Classes)
    Regulation of Issue of Community Certificates Act 1993 (Act 16/1993).
  2. Andhra Pradesh Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Backward Classes - Issue of
    Community, Nativity and Date of Birth Certificates Rules, 1997.
  3. Government orders issued from time to time amending the list of Castes.

IMPORTANT SECTIONS OF ACT.

Sec-3: Any person belonging to SC, ST and BC classes shall file an application before competent authority.

Sec-4:The competent authority shall issue community certificate basing on enquiry and shall accept or reject the claim.

Sec-5:Empowers District Collector to cancel false Community certificates after the due enquiry prescribed under Act.

Sec-6:Burden of proof always lies on the claimant in all types of enquires related to Community Certificates.

Sec-7:APPEAL: Any person aggrieved by the rejection order as per Sec.4, issued by competent authority may
prefer appeal before “District Collector” within 30 days from the receipt of order.
REVIEW: Any person aggrieved by the orders of the appellated authority may prefer appeal to the Government
within the 30 days from the date of Gazette publication by “District Collector”.

Sec-8:POWER OF REVISION BY GOVERNMENT: The Government may at any time either Suo-motu (or) an application
may review the orders / decision passed by any authority in this regard.

Sec-10:PENALTIES: If any person obtains caste certificate in fraudulent means is punishable with imprisonment
for a term not less than 6 months but which may extend upto 2 years with fine.

Sec-13:If any competent authority issues a false certificate intentionally is also punishable with imprisonment for
a term not less than 6 months but which may extend upto 2 years with fine.

IMPORTANT RULES:

Rule-4(a):Every applicant /Parent/Guardian shall submit application in Form I / II before competent authority
for issuance of caste certificate.
Form I:For Schedule Tribes (ST).
Form II: Schedule Caste (SC) and Backward Classes (BC).

Rule-4(b):the applicant is minor parent/ guardian shall submit application.

Rule-5(a):the procedure for verification of claims and issuance of certificates.
On accept of application competent authority shall verify the correctness of documents /evidence
and if satisfied with correctness of claim, community certificate must be issued in
Form-IIIspecifying caste / sub caste.

Rule-5(b):competent authority feels that further enquiry necessary to prove the caste status,
he may issue notice in Form-IV to applicant to appear before him along with relevant records.

Rule-5(f): competent authority shall requisition the services of Revenue Inspector (RI) and
Village Revenue Officer (VRO) in the enquiry.

Rule-5(i): competent authority may refer doubtful claims to “Chairman, District Level Scrutiny Committee”
i.e., Joint Collector for recommendation to finalize the claim.

Rule-8(a): Level Scrutiny Committee with 5 members under chairmanship of Joint Collector deals with
the doubtful cases referred by competent authorities under Rule 5(i).

Rule-8(d): DLSC conducts enquiry by issuing notices in Form-V to applicant and collects the documentary
evidences/records statements from applicant/persons and after giving proper opportunity and due enquiry finalizes
it’s recommendations to competent authority.

Rule-9: CLAIMS:The District Collector when receives a written complaint on false community certificates refers
the matter to District Level Scrutiny Committee and DLSC in turn issues Form-VI notice and conducts enquiry prescribed
under Rule-8(d). Furnishes it’s findings to District Collector within 60 days. The District Collector shall pass order
for cancellation of such fraudulent claims.

PROCEDURE FOR ISSUANCE OF COMMUINITY CERTIFICATES (RULE-5)

  • Application for issuance of Community, Nativity and Date of Birth Certificate shall be submitted
    in the prescribed Form- I / II to the MRO/RDO/Collector
    (Competent Authority prescribed under Annexure-I appended to the Rules).
  • The competent authority shall verify the following information/ documents/ evidences furnished by applicant.
    1. Application Form.
    2. Previously issued Caste Certificate issued to the family members.
    3. SSC Marks Memo/ DOB Extract/ Transfer Certificate.
    4. 1 to 10th study certificates or DOB issued by the GP/MA.
    5. Ration card/ EPIC card/ Aadhaar card.
    6. Schedule I to IV (for Schedule Tribes).
    7. Caste certificate of any relative having blood relation with applicant from
      Paternal side and proof of such relation with Genealogical tree/chart.
  • The Village Revenue Officer and Revenue Inspector will conduct field inspection
    or local inquiry i.e., records statements from the applicant, parents, Village elders
    who are having the knowledge of social status of the applicant/ conduct panchanama to verify
    correctness of the caste claim and finally recommends to the competent authority.
  • If the competent authority feels further enquiry is necessary may issue notice in Form-IV
    to the applicant for obtaining additional information regarding the applicant such as anthropology
    and ethnological traits, deity, rituals, customs, mode of marriage, death ceremonies/method of burial
    of dead bodies etc.,
  • For certain castes or in specific cases in which the Revenue Divisional Officer or Collector is competent
    authority the Tahsildar shall forward the application to them after due enquiry for final review and approval.
  • Community, Nativity and Date of Birth Certificates shall be issued in Form-III within 30 days by competent
    authority if he is satisfied with the correctness of the information/documents/evidence furnished by the applicant.
  • The competent authority may refer certain cases to Tribal Welfare Department in respect of Tribal communities
    who are not Traditional inhabitants in order to confirm or reject the claim
  • In doubtful cases, the competent authority shall refer the matter to DLSC and based on the recommendation
    given by the DLSC the competent authority shall conform or reject the claim.

IMPORTANT POINTS TO BE OBSERVED WHILE ISSUING CASTE CERTIFICATES:

  1. COMPETENT AUTHORITY:

    Government notifies the competent authority for issuance of community certificates.
    The component authorities or specified in Annexure-I of appended to the above rules.

      Example:-
    • For all BC communities, “Tahsildar” is competent for issuance of community certificates.
    • For issuance of community certificates to Konda Kapus, Konda Reddies, Revenue Divisional Officer/Sub-Collector is competent.
    • For issuance of community certificates to Bariki community District Collector is competent.

    Note:The competent authority shall verify about the competency for issuance of community certificates
    in the Annexure-I appended to the rules before issuance.

  2. Competent authority shall have the updated list of SC, ST and BC communities specific to the Sate as there are
    separate lists to the different States. The Government may alter the list from time to time.
  3. The competent authority shall have knowledge about the area restrictions pertaining to certain communities.
  4. The competent authority shall verify the contents of the application and signature of the applicant/parents/guardian
    before issuing of certificates
  5. The Competent authority shall verify the following ‘during’ enquiry.
    • (a) Information/documents/evidence such as school records/ birth registration certificate etc.
    • (b) May record statements from any person who is having knowledge of social state of claimant.
    • (c) May study anthropology and ethnological traits, deity, rituals, customs, mode of marriage,
      death ceremonies/method of burial of dead bodies etc., pertaining claimant.

GUIDELINES ISSUED BY GOVERNMENT OF INDIA TO BE FOLLOWED BEFORE ISSUING SC, ST COMMUNITY CERTIFICATES

  1. GENERAL: (APPLICABLE IN ALL CASES):

    2. Where a person claims to belong to a Scheduled Caste or a Scheduled Tribe by birth it should be verified: --

    • (i) that the person and his/her parents actually belong to the community claimed;
    • (ii) that this community is included in the Presidential Orders specifying the Scheduled
      Castes and Scheduled Tribes in relation to the concerned State.
    • (iii) that the person belongs to that State and to the area within that State in respect
      of which the community has been scheduled;
    • (iv) if the person claims to be a Scheduled Castes, he/she should profess either the Hindu
      or the Sikh, Buddhist religion;
    • (v) if the person claims to be a Scheduled Tribe, he/she may profess any religion.

  2. CASES OF MIGRATION:
    • (i) Where a person migrates from the portion of the State in respect of which his/her community is
      scheduled to another part of the same State in respect of which his/her community is not scheduled,
      he/she will continue to be deemed to be a member of the Scheduled Caste or the Scheduled Tribe, as the
      case may be, in relation to that State;
    • (ii) Where a person migrates from one State to another, he/she can claim to belong to a Scheduled Caste
      or a Scheduled Tribe only in relation to the State to which he/she originally belonged and not in respect
      of the State to which he/she has migrated.
  3. CLAIMS THROUGH MARRIAGE:

    4. The guiding principle is that no person who was not a Scheduled Caste or a Scheduled Tribe by birth will be deemed
    to be a member of a Scheduled Caste or a Scheduled Tribe merely because he or she had married a person belonging to a
    Scheduled Caste or a Scheduled Tribe.

    Similarly a person who is a member of a Scheduled Caste or a Scheduled Tribe would continue to be a member of that
    Scheduled Caste or Scheduled Tribe as the case may be, even after his or her marriage with a person who does not belong
    to a Scheduled Caste or a Scheduled Tribe.

  4. CASES OF CONVERSION AND RE-CONVERSION

    • (i) Where a Scheduled Caste person gets converted to a religion other than Hinduism or Sikhism and then reconverts
      himself back to Hinduism or Sikhism, he/she will be deemed to have reverted to his/her original Scheduled Caste,
      if he/she is accepted by the members of that particular caste as one among them.
    • (ii) In the case of a descendent of a Scheduled Caste convert, the mere fact of conversion to Hinduism or Sikhism
      will not be sufficient to entitle him to be regarded as a member of the Scheduled Caste to which his/her forefathers
      belonged. It will have to be established that such a convert has been accepted by members of the caste claimed as one
      among themselves and has thus become a member of that caste.

  5. CASES OF ADOPTION:

    6. Great care has to be exercised in dealing with cases where a person claims to be a Scheduled Caste on the ground that
    he/she has been adopted by a Scheduled Caste person. The validity of the adoption has to be clearly established before
    any caste certificate can be given. It is for the party to prove his/her claim by cogent and reliable evidence. And adoption
    must be a valid one under the requirements given in section 6 to 11 of the Hindu Adoption and Maintenance Act, 1956.

  6. CASTE STATUS OF OFFSPRING OF INTER-CASTE MARRIED PERSONS:

    7. To determine the caste status of the offspring of the couple where one the spouses is a member of Schedule Caste / Schedule Tribe
    the crucial test to determine the caste status of the offspring is that he/she shall be brought up on the Schedule Caste or
    Schedule Tribe community and shall be treated as a member of the such community and shall be accepted by the community.

  7. CASTE STATUS OF OFFSPRING OF DIVORCED/SEPARATED/SINGLE WOMEN:

    8. To determine the caste status of the offspring of the Divorced / Separated / Single Women it is established that the child
    has been brought up in the surroundings of a notified community to which the mother belongs to and has suffered the
    deprivations, indignities, humilities and handicaps like any other member of that community and he or she was always
    treated as a member of the mother's community not only by that community but by people outside the community as well,
    then such a child of separated/divorced/single women has to be treated as a member of the Scheduled Caste community
    and would be entitled to receive benefits as such.

CASE STUDIES:

  1. V.V.GIRI Vs D.S.Dora case (1959): This Judgement by Supreme Court concluded the crucial test to determine the caste status
    of the child born to a couple where one of the spouses is a member of Schedule Caste. The caste status of such child shall be
    determined on the basis that the surroundings he has been brought up and basing on the acceptance by the Schedule Caste
    community as a member of their community.
  2. Mrs.Valsamma Paul Vs Cochin University (1996): The Supreme Court affirmed the position that reservation benefits under
    Articles 15(4) and 16(4) are intended solely for individuals who belong to socially and educationally backward classes by
    birth or have inherently suffered the associated disadvantages. The court held that marriage into a Backward Class does not
    automatically confer eligibility for reservation benefits meant for that class. Valsamma Paul's appointment was thus
    invalidated as she remained a member of the Forward Class despite her marital alliance with a Backward Class individual.
  3. A.Prathyusha Vs Registrar, NTR University of Health (1999):The Scheduled Caste and Scheduled Tribe Certificate is not a
    bounty to be distributed. To sustain the claim, one must show that he/she suffered disabilities - socially, economically
    and educationally cumulatively. The concerned authority, before whom such claim is made, is duty bound to satisfy itself
    that the applicant suffered disabilities socially, economically and educationally before such certificate is issued.
    Any concerned authority issuing such certificates in a routine manner would be committing the dereliction of Constitutional duty.
  4. Anjan Kumar Vs Union of India (2006): The Supreme Court dismissed the appeal filed by Shri Anjan Kumar, quashing his ST
    certificate issued in 1992. The court held that Kumar, being the offspring of a tribal woman married to a non-tribal man
    (Kayastha), could not claim ST status. The judgment emphasized that mere marital affiliation does not confer tribal status
    unless the individual is raised in a tribal environment and subjected to the disabilities that the reservation system aims
    to address. Additionally, the court highlighted the fraudulent implications of obtaining ST certificates through misrepresentation,
    stressing the need to protect genuine benefits intended for truly disadvantaged communities.
  5. Rameshbhai Dabhai Naika Vs State of Gujarat (2012): This Judgement by the Supreme Court concluded that practice of the
    practice of issuance of caste certificate based on the caste of father is not without exceptions. In certain cases, where it
    is established that the children have grown up in a notified community or tribe and have suffered all the disadvantages and
    deprivations belonging to such community, the office order could not come in the way of issuance of caste certificate certifying
    that the applicants belongs to caste of their mother.

ANDHRA PRADESH STATE - BACKWARD CLASSES (BC) CERTIFICATES

  • The Government of Andhra Pradesh appointed “Anantha Raman Commission” in the year 1968 to prepare a list of Backward Classes
    in the State as socially and educationally Backward Classes.
  • The Commission after studying the different types of communities submitted a report with a list consisting of 93 classes to
    be included in the Backward Classes.
  • The commission sub-divided the communities into 4 categories (A,B,C,D) basing on their backwardness.
  • The Government accepted the criteria adopted by the Commission and issued G.O.Ms. No.1793 dated 23.09.1970 declaring that the
    93 castes and communities specified by the Commission to be socially and educationally backward for making 25% reservation for them.
  • The Government issued G.O.Ms.No.23 BCW(C2)Dept., dated 07.07.2007 in ordered to provide reservations to the Economically and Socially
    Backward Muslims by creating 5th category BC-E with 14 types of Muslim communities.
  • The Government from time to time amended the above list by adding or deleting certain communities. At present 138 Backward Classes
    are existing in Andhra Pradesh.
    The Details of the Backward Classes in Andhra Pradesh are as follows:

ANDHRA PRADESH STATE – OTHER BACKWARD CLASSES (OBC)-NON CREAMY LAYER (NCL) CERTIFICATES

  • The Government of India appointed “Mandal Commission” to identify the socially and Educationally Backward Classes
    for providing reservations in Civil posts related to Central Government Institutions and Public sector units.
  • Based on the recommendations The Government of India provided 27% of reservation to Other Backward Classes (OBC)
    in Civil posts and Services related to Government of India.
  • The Supreme Court in Indira Sawhney Vs. Others Vs. Union of India (1990) delivered judgement and recommended criteria
    for exclusion of the Socially advanced persons / sections from getting reservations and introduced the concept of Creamy Layer.
  • The candidates who are seeking reservation under OBC category have to submit OBC-NCL certificate for appointment of posts
    under the Government of India and Educational institutions.
  • The competent authorities for issuing of OBC-NCL is the Revenue Officer not below the rank of “Tahsildar”.
  • The competent authorities shall specify OBC status and also to certify that the candidate does not belong to the Creamy Layer in
    the OBC-NCL certificate.

IMPORTANT POINTS TO BE REMEMBERED WHILE ISSUING OBC/NCL CERTIFICATES:

  1. The Government of India prepares list of OBC communities with the recommendations made by National Commission for Backward Classes(NCBC).
  2. The OBC lists remains specific to the every state
  3. The central lists of OBC changes from time to time as per the recommendations made by the NCBC. Certain OBC may be deleted or added to the Central list of OBC.
  4. At present there are 104 Other Backward Classes notified by the Government of India for the State of Andhra Pradesh.
  5. Certain Backward Classes of a state may not be notified in central list of OBC.
    Example:- The 14 Muslim communities notified under BC-E category in Andhra Pradesh are not notified in central list of OBC.
    Therefore, the communities mentioned under BC-E category are not eligible for getting OBC-NCL certificates. However they may
    be considered under EWS Category as per their eligibility.
  6. The competent authority shall specify the resolution number of the OBC in OBC-NCL certificate which was mentioned against to the caste in Central OBC list.
    Example:- For Dommara community the resolution number specified in Central OBC list at Sl.No.7 is 12011/68/93-BCC(C ) dt:10/09/1993.
  7. The competent authority shall specify the creamy layer status in OBC-NCL as per the exclusion criteria mentioned here under.

POINTS TO BE REMEMBER WHILE DETERMINING THE CREAMY LAYER STATUS:

  1. The creamy layer status of the applicant must be determined with the status of parents only but not with the applicant status.
  2. In case of married women, the creamy layer status must be determined with the status of parents only but not with the status of the husband.
  3. The creamy layer status of the employee must be determined with the initial appointment only but not with the status of salaried income.
  4. The Agriculture income shall not be considered to determine the creamy layer status
  5. The income and wealth test i.e., persons having gross annual income of Rs.8 lakhs and above is only applicable to professional
    class/engaged and trade industry mentioned under Category-IV, Category-V(B,C) and Category-VII. The income and wealth test is not
    applicable to the employees of Central and State services.

ANDHRA PRADESH GOVERNMENT–BACK WARD CLASSES CREAMY LAYER CERTIFICATES:

  1. The Government of Andhra Pradesh issued GO.Ms.No.3 dated 04.04.2006 for issuance of BC Creamy layer certificate for
    getting reservations in Civil posts and for seeking admissions in educational institutions under State Government.
  2. The Government of Andhra Pradesh have adopted the similar criteria as fixed by the Government of India for determination of the Creamy layer status.
  3. The Government of Andhra Pradesh have issued G.O.Ms.No.26 Backward Classes Welfare(C) Department dated 09.12.2013 in
    which raised income limit to Rs.6 lakhs per annum for determining creamy layer among the Backward Classes in Andhra Pradesh State.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS ON OTHER BACKWARD CLASSES (OBC):

Question-1.If the land/property (more than threshold limit) is in the name of parents and there are more than one child and the property
has not been distributed, then in that situation, can one of the children apply for EWS certificate?

Answer:No. Para 4.2 and 4.3 of said O.M. dated 31.01.2019 provides that the property held by a "Family" in different locations or different
places/cities would be clubbed while applying the land or property holding test to determine EWS status and the term "Family" for this purpose
will include the person who seeks benefit of reservation, his/her parents and siblings below the age of 18 years as also his/her spouse and children
below the age of 18 years. Property beyond threshold limit tantamount to disqualification for applying to Income and Asset Certificate in respect of
any of the children.

Question-2. In case agricultural land/residential flat/plot of parents is located in more than one State/District or within the same
State/District, all such land/properties will be clubbed or not?

Answer: Yes. Para 4.2 of said O.M. dated 31.01.2019 provides that, the property held by a "Family" in different locations or
different places/cities would be clubbed while applying the land or property holding test to determine EWS status. Person can apply for Income
and Asset Certificate in the district of his permanent address

Question-3.Does the residential flat/plot as classified under Para 4.1 of DoPT OM, dated 31.1.2019, Includes both commercial and residential property?

Answer:Para 4.1 of the OM, dated 31.1.2019, speaks of residential property. However, exclusion of commercial
property will result in injustice. Hence, the definition of residential flat/plot in the OM also includes the commercial property.
